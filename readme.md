# Timeline

Timeline is an open-source platform for long-term cultural and historical preservation, designed around principles of openness, verifiability, and community ownership.

The project explores how decentralized systems and open-source development practices can be applied to preserve cultural knowledge in a durable, censorship-resistant way—especially for communities whose histories are at risk of being lost or distorted.

## Motivation

Cultural records today are largely stored on centralized platforms that prioritize short-term engagement, monetization, and algorithmic visibility. This creates a fragile environment for preserving languages, oral histories, traditions, and community knowledge.

Timeline aims to provide an alternative: a public-good platform where cultural data can be documented, referenced, and maintained over time without dependence on a single organization or platform.

## Project Goals

- Build an open-source foundation for cultural and historical documentation
- Enable communities to publish and curate their own cultural records
- Prioritize long-term durability over short-term engagement metrics
- Encourage collaboration, research, and contribution from developers and educators
- Align with open-source and Bitcoin-adjacent development values

## Current Status

Timeline is currently in early development and research phase.

The focus at this stage includes:
- System design and architecture
- Data modeling for cultural records
- Documentation and contributor onboarding
- Exploration of decentralized and verifiable storage patterns

This repository will evolve as development progresses.
